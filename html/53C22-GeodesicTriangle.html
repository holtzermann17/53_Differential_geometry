geodesic triangle


Let M be a differentiable manifold (at least two times differentiable) and A,B,C∈M (not necessarily distinct). Let x1,x2,x3∈[0,∞). Let γ1:[0,x1]→M, γ2:[0,x2]→M, and γ3:[0,x3]→M be geodesics such that all of the following hold:

  • •

    γ1⁢(0)=A;

  • •

    γ1⁢(x1)=B;

  • •

    γ2⁢(0)=A;

  • •

    γ2⁢(x2)=C;

  • •

    γ3⁢(0)=B;

  • •

    γ3⁢(x3)=C.

Then the figure determined by γ1, γ2, and γ3 is a geodesic triangle.

Note that a geodesic triangle need not be a triangle. For example, in ℝ2, if A=(0,0), B=(1,2), and C=(3,6), then the geodesic triangle determined by A, B, and C is {(x,2⁢x):x∈[0,3]}, which is not a triangle.
