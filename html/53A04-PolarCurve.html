polar curve


Polar curves are plane curves in ℝ2 that are expressed in polar coordinates (r,θ). The two simplest polar curves are obtained when one of the two coordinates is set to be a constant. If the first coordinate is set to a constant r, we have a circle with radius |r|, or a point when r=0. When the second coordinate is the constant instead, say c, we have a straight line through the (polar) origin, with slope = tan⁡c.
