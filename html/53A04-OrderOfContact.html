order of contact


Suppose that A and B are smooth curves in ℝn which pass through a common point P. We say that A and B have zeroth order contact if their tangents at P are distinct.

Suppose that A and B are tangent at P. We may then set up a coordinate system in which P is the origin and the x1 axis is tangent to both curves. By the implicit function theorem, there will be a neighborhood of P such that A can be described parametrically as xi=fi⁢(x1) with i=2,…,n and B can be described parametrically as xi=gi⁢(x1) with i=2,…,n. We then define the order of contact of A and B at P to be the largest integer m such that all partial derivatives of fi and gi of order not greater than m at P are equal.
