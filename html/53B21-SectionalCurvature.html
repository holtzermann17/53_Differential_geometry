sectional curvature


Let M be a Riemannian manifold. Let p be a point in M and let S be a two-dimensional subspace of Tp⁢M. Then the sectional curvature of S at p is defined as

K⁢(S)=g⁢(R⁢(x,y)⁢x,y)g⁢(x,x)⁢g⁢(y,y)-g⁢(x,y)2

where x,y span S, g is the metric tensor and R is the Riemann’s curvature tensor.

This is a natural generalization of the classical Gaussian curvature for surfaces.
