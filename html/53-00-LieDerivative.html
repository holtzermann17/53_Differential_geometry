Lie derivative


Let M be a smooth manifold, X a vector field on M, and T a tensor on M. Then the Lie derivative ℒX⁢T of T along X is a tensor of the same rank as T defined as

ℒX⁢T=dd⁢t⁢(ρt*⁢(T))|t=0

where ρ is the flow of X, and ρt* is pullback by ρt.

The Lie derivative is a notion of directional derivative for tensors. Intuitively, this is the change in T in the direction of X.

If X and Y are vector fields, then ℒX⁢Y=[X,Y], the standard Lie bracket of vector fields.
