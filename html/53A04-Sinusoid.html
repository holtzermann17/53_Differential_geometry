sinusoid


A sinusoid is a curve of the form

ℝ → ℝ2
t ↦ (t,sin⁡k⁢t),

where k>0 is a parameter determining the oscillation.

The basic sinusoid, the curve

y=sin⁡x

in the x⁢y-plane, oscillates periodically with the period of sine (http://planetmath.org/ComplexSineAndCosine), 2⁢π, as x increases.

  • •

    On the interval  [0,π2],  the curve is ascending because the derivative of sine (http://planetmath.org/ComplexSineAndCosine), cos⁡x, is positive for acute angles x.

  • •

    Consequently, on the interval  [π2,π], the supplement formula  sin⁡(π-x)=sin⁡x  tells that the sinusoid is descending.

  • •

    Thus we get on the whole interval  [0,π]  a cap-formed (⌢) arc.

  • •

    Because sine is an odd function (http://planetmath.org/ComplexSineAndCosine), we have on the interval  [-π, 0]  the of the cap, a cup-formed (⌣) arc.

  • •

    All in all, on the period interval  [-π,π]  the sinusoid consists of the consecutive cup and cap, together a lying-S formed (∽) arc.

  • •

    The same is repeated on each other period interval  [(2⁢n-1)⁢π,(2⁢n+1)⁢π]  where  n∈ℤ.
