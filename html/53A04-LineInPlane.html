line in plane


Equation of a line

Suppose a,b,c∈ℝ. Then the set of points (x,y) in the plane that satisfy

a⁢x+b⁢y+c= 0,

where a and b can not be both 0, is an (infinite) line.

The value of y when x=0, if it exists, is called the y-intercept. Geometrically, if d is the y-intercept, then (0,d) is the point of intersection of the line and the y-axis. The y-intercept exists iff the line is not parallel to the y-axis. The x-intercept is defined similarly.

If b≠0, then the above equation of the line can be rewritten as

y=m⁢x+d.

This is called the slope-intercept form of a line, because both the slope and the y-intercept are easily identifiable in the equation. The slope is m and the y-intercept is d.

Three finite points (x1,y1), (x2,y2), (x3,y3) in ℝ2 are collinear if and only if the following determinant vanishes:

|x1x2x3y1y2y3111|=0.

Therefore, the line through distinct points (x1,y1) and (x2,y2) has equation

|x1x2xy1y2y111|=0,

or more simply

(y1-y2)⁢x+(x2-x1)⁢y+y2⁢x1-x2⁢y1=0.

Line segment

Let p1=(x1,y1) and p2=(x2,y2) be distinct points in ℝ2. The closed line segement generated by these points is the set

{p∈ℝ2∣p=t⁢p1+(1-t)⁢p2, 0≤t≤1}.
