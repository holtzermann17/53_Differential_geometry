vector field along a curve


Let M be a differentiable manifold and γ:[a,b]→M be a differentiable curve in M. Then a vector field along γ is a differentiable map Γ:[a,b]→T⁢M, the tangent bundle of M, which projects to γ under the natural projection π:T⁢M→M. That is, it assigns to each point t0∈[a,b] a vector tangent to M at the point γ⁢(t), in a continuous manner. A good example of a vector field along a curve is the speed vector γ˙. This is the pushforward of the constant vector field dd⁢t by γ, i.e., at t0, it is the derivation γ˙⁢(f)=dd⁢t⁢(f∘γ)|t=t0.
