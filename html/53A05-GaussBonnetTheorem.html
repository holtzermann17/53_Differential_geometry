Gauss-Bonnet theorem


(Carl Friedrich Gauss and Pierre Ossian Bonnet) Given a two-dimensional compact Riemannian manifold M with boundary, Gaussian curvature of points G and geodesic curvature of points gx on the boundary ∂⁡M, it is the case that

∫MG⁢𝑑A+∫∂⁡Mgx⁢𝑑s=2⁢π⁢χ⁢(M),

where χ⁢(M) is the Euler characteristic of the manifold, d⁢A denotes the measure with respect to area, and d⁢s denotes the measure with respect to arclength on the boundary. This theorem expresses a topological invariant in terms of geometrical information.
