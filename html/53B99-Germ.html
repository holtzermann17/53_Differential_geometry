Definition 1 (Germ).

Let M and N be manifolds and x∈M. We consider all smooth mappings f:Uf→N, where Uf is some open neighborhood of x in M. We define an equivalence relation on the set of mappings considered, and we put f⁢∼𝑥⁢g if there is some open neighborhood V of x with f|V=g|V. The equivalence class of a mapping f is called the germ of f at x, denoted by f¯ or, sometimes, g⁢e⁢r⁢mx⁢f, and we write

f¯:(M,x)→(N,f⁢(x))

Remark 1.

Germs arise naturally in differential topolgy. It is very convenient when dealing with derivatives at the point x, as every mapping in a germ will have the same derivative values and properties in x, and hence can be identified for such purposes: every mapping in a germ gives rise to the same tangent vector of M at x.
