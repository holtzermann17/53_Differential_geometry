Darboux’s theorem (symplectic geometry)


If (M,ω) is a 2⁢n-dimensional symplectic manifold, and m∈M, then there exists a neighborhood U of m with a coordinate chart

x=(x1,…,x2⁢n):U→ℝ2⁢n,

such that

ω=∑i=1nd⁢xi∧d⁢xn+i.

These are called canonical or Darboux coordinates. On U, ω is the pullback by X of the standard symplectic form on ℝ2⁢n, so x is a symplectomorphism. Darboux’s theorem implies that there are no local invariants in symplectic geometry, unlike in Riemannian geometry, where there is curvature.
