symplectic vector space


A symplectic vector space (V,ω) is a finite dimensional real vector space V equipped with an alternating non-degenerate 2-tensor, i.e., a bilinear map ω:V×V→ℝ that satisfies the following properties:

  1. 1.

    Alternating: For all v,w∈V, ω⁢(v,w)=-ω⁢(w,v).

  2. 2.

    Non-degenerate: If ω⁢(v,w)=0 for all w∈V, then v=0.

The tensor ω is called a for V.

A linear automorphism T∈Aut⁢(V) is called linear symplectomorphism when T*⁢ω=ω, i.e.

ω⁢(T⁢v,T⁢w)=ω⁢(v,w) ∀v,w∈W.

Linear symplectomorphisms of (V,ω) form a group (under composition of linear map) that is denoted by Sp⁢(V,ω).

One can show that a symplectic vector space is always even dimensional [1].
